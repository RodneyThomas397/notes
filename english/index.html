Course Objectives

This course has been designed to provide you with an intensive study of literature, writing, vocabulary, and grammar. Students are expected to be able to read independently, complete thorough analyses of literature, and write both analytically and creatively.

Grading Criteria

The Rules

  1. Be respectful – This classroom is meant to be a safe area, and this is not possible if students are being disrespectful to the teacher, other students, or themselves. Treat others with respect and sensitivity because only such civil behavior will produce the kind of atmosphere where all students are capable of learning from each other.
  2. Be willing – You must be willing to devote regular time to the class. You will be faced with challenging reading and writing assignments, and I expect you to approach each task with an open mind.
  3. Be on time - Wasted time means wasted work and instruction time. While we are learning remotely, you must be logged in to assigned Google Meets on time. When we are learning in the classroom, you must be in your seat when the bell rings, and you cannot pack up early or line up by the door.
  4. Be prepared – You must consistently come to class prepared and turn in all work on time.
  5. Be honest – In my classroom, there is a zero-tolerance policy for any form of dishonesty. This may include, but certainly is not limited to, lying, cheating, and plagiarism.
  6. Be resourceful - It is imperative you are able to work independently and to problem solve any issues or challenges that may arise, carefully scheduling your time to complete long-term assignments.
  7. Be ready to participate – I want to see that you are all learning. Please participate in classroom discussions and activities.
  8. Be open to feedback – Please understand you will be graded by rigorous standards. Reflect on your own progress as a reader and writer, and embrace feedback, both from the teacher and other students.

Cellphone/Electronic Devices Policy

The use of electronic devices and other technology at school is a privilege, not a right. Students are prohibited from using electronic devices, except as provided herein.

An electronic device includes but is not limited to, the following: cell phone, smartphone, audio or video recording device, personal digital assistant (PDA), ipod©, iPad©, laptop computer, tablet computer, or other similar electronic devices.

During instructional time, which includes class periods, student-owned electronic devices must be kept powered off and out of sight unless: (a) permission is granted by an administrator, teacher or school staff member; (b) use of the device is provided in a student’s individualized plan (IEP); or (c) it is needed in an emergency that threatens the safety of students, staff, or other individuals.

Students are allowed to use electronic devices during non-instructional time, which is defined as before and after school, during the student’s lunch period, and during passing periods.

Electronic devices may never be used in any manner that disrupts the educational environment, violates student conduct rules, or violates the rights of others. This includes, but is not limited to, the following: (1) using the device to take photographs in locker rooms or bathrooms; (2) cheating; and (3) creating, sending, sharing, viewing, receiving, or possessing an indecent visual depiction or non-consensual dissemination of private sexual images (i.e., sexting). The school and school district are not responsible for the loss, theft, or damage to any electronic device brought to school.

Student Expectations for Hybrid/Remote Learning

Remote instruction lessons may be recorded by the teacher for viewing by students assigned to the class. The recordings are intended to only capture the images and voice of the teacher.  However, there may be occasions in which a student’s voice is momentarily captured. Students may not record classroom instruction or activities using their computer, cell phone, or other devices. Instructional videos may be posted to the class webpage for access by students.

Be patient with yourselves and the school staff; we are all learning together.
