Tech Support Internship Course Syllabus 2022/23

Welcome to Technical Support Internship (TSI)!  TSI is a student-run help desk that provides ﬁrst-line technical support to students, support staff, and teachers.  You will also get the chance to explore different technology-related pathways as well.  This will be an exciting year!

As a TSI Intern you will:

  • troubleshoot and repair computer/Chromebook hardware and software issues
  • demonstrate an understanding of ticketing systems by creating, editing and updating work tickets
  • provide excellent customer service to students and teachers by using proper greetings, documenting issues, providing information regarding repairs and fees, and solving issues efficiently
  • demonstrate proficiency using Google Apps and other systems

On a daily basis, you will be expected to:

  • arrive to class on time
  • assist students and teachers in an efficient manner
  • use appropriate social and customer service skills
  • remain on task while working on individual pathways
  • manage the front desk station
  • show professionalism when servicing tickets outside of the TSI classroom

TSI interns grades may be negatively affected by the following behaviors:

  • failing to adhere to rules & procedures directly relating to the TSI classroom and TSI course objectives
  • inappropriate use of classroom resources including, but not limited to, the computers and internet
  • excessive absences that negatively impact performance in TSI
  • behaviors and/or actions deemed inappropriate by the TSI instructors or school administration

The learning experiences in TSI are unique and valuable.  If you have any questions, please contact your TSI instructor, Alex Mann - alexander.mann@chsd117.org; 847-395-9951

Community High School District 117 Policies and General Academic Expectations

STUDENT HANDBOOK

  • All D117 classrooms abide by the criteria outlined in the Community High School District 117 student handbooks.  

ATTENDANCE, ABSENCES, AND TARDIES

  • If a student is absent, it is the student’s responsibility to make arrangements with teachers to make up for the missed work.
  • A student entering a class with a valid pass will not be counted as tardy.  Students tardy for any other reason will be marked as tardy and remain in class. At the start of each semester, tardy Interventions to class will be handled as follows:
  • First Tardy: Classroom teacher will record and conference with the student
  • Second Tardy: Classroom teacher will record and assign one teacher detention
  • Third Tardy: Classroom teacher will record, assign one teacher detention and notify the student’s parent/guardian
  • Subsequent Tardies: Student is to remain in class and a referral will be forwarded to the Dean of Students
  • Students more than 10 minutes late to class may be referred to the Dean of Students.  Students who are persistently tardy to class may be considered insubordinate and the appropriate discipline will be assigned.

ALLERGIES

  • When our curriculum involves the handling and/or consumption of food, staff will notify students and parents/guardians in advance. Accommodations and/or alternative assignments will be made available for students with documented allergies.  

CONTROVERSIAL CURRICULUM

  • CHSD 117 understands that as a parent or guardian, you have the right to excuse your child from certain topics in our curricula. Please review the topics in the various syllabi provided to your child. Each student will be included in learning all content found in each syllabus unless you request that he/she be opted out during that specific time of learning. If opted out, your student will be provided with an alternative assignment. If you want to opt out your child from a specific topic, please contact your child’s teacher or the department’s chair within two weeks of the start of the semester.

USE OF ELECTRONIC DEVICES

  • Use of electronic devices are only permitted in the classroom at the discretion of the teacher.
  • The inappropriate use of the District’s electronic network, as defined in the School Board’s Access to Electronic Networks Policy and its administrative procedures and implementing rules and regulations, may result in the loss of the privilege to use this resource or other disciplinary action. This responsibility includes the proper use of mobile devices and these devices’ capability of electronic recordings. No electronic recordings of any sort are allowed without the knowledge and consent of the classroom teacher. Remember that you are legally responsible for your actions/your child’s actions.
  • Classroom activities may be photographed or recorded by the teacher for viewing by students assigned to the class.  The recordings are intended to only capture the images and voice of the teacher.  However, there may be occasions in which a student’s voice is momentarily captured.
