ACHS Social Studies Department Mission Statement:

The ACHS Social Studies Department is committed to providing a strong foundation for students to succeed in education and in furthering their academic goals for the future.  Students will be given the opportunity to gain the necessary knowledge, skills and attitudes to understand, respect, and practice the ways of a scholar and citizen.  Most importantly, the ACHS Social Studies Department is committed to shaping and developing productive members of society.  We intend to have a lasting, positive impact upon students and consider it a privilege to do so!

Course Overview:

This course provides an introduction to the Social Studies Department and is designed to offer you a deeper knowledge of the historical and cultural beliefs of a variety of regions throughout the world.  This course will utilize a multitude of academic skills that are necessary to meet and exceed the demands of subsequent Social Studies Department courses and its progressing curriculum.  This Global Studies course will focus primarily on case studies of civilizations from prehistoric times through the present era revolving around the major geographical regions.  Our course themes will be approached with a global lens in mind as we seek to sample multiple cultures all the while understanding the significant influence Western thinking has had in forming our collective worldview.

Late Work Policy—Daily Assignments

All assignments will be accepted for full credit up until the cumulative assessment for that respective unit. Missing assignments will be entered as a zero in the gradebook until students turn in the assignment. It will be EXTREMELY beneficial for the student to complete work as it is assigned. Students will fall behind in class if they do not keep on top of their assignments. Students will be given no credit for assignments turned in after the cumulative assessment. Students are still encouraged to complete the assignment, regardless of how late it is, for their own educational benefit regardless of points.

Late Work Policy—Large Assignments and Projects

Ten percentage points (10%) will be taken off each day a project is late.  After the fourth day no credit will be given.

Absences: Students are responsible for obtaining any work missed and making it up in the time allowed. Students have one day to make up their work for each day they were absent (this applies only to excused absences). See late work policy in the school handbook if you have any questions!

Homework assignments: Students should obtain a copy of the assignment or a description of what was assigned from Mrs. Ruley before or after class. It is not appropriate to interrupt the class to find out what was missed. You can also email Mrs. Ruley to obtain the work missed if you are unable to find the assignment on google classroom.

Major Tests: I will put all missing tests in the testing center. Major tests need to be made up within one week of the date of the test. If you do not make up the test within a week, you will receive a zero.

ATTENDANCE, ABSENCES, AND TARDIES

  • If a student is absent, it is the student’s responsibility to make arrangements with teachers to make up for the missed work.
  • A student entering a class with a valid pass will not be counted as tardy.  Students tardy for any other reason will be marked as tardy and remain in class. At the start of each semester, tardy Interventions to class will be handled as follows:
  • First Tardy: Classroom teacher will record and conference with the student
  • Second Tardy: Classroom teacher will record and assign one teacher detention
  • Third Tardy: Classroom teacher will record, assign one teacher detention and notify the student’s parent/guardian
  • Subsequent Tardies: Student is to remain in class and a referral will be forwarded to the Dean of Students
  • Students more than 10 minutes late to class may be referred to the Dean of Students.  Students who are persistently tardy to class may be considered insubordinate and the appropriate discipline will be assigned.
